About the Project


eBuddy is a social networking app that focuses on helping people who struggle to find the right person, to do the right thing, at the right time to establish genuine and meaningful social connections with others who share the same interests and passions with them from a virtual world to real life.


Role & Responsibilities: As one of the main UX designers, I have been involved in all phases of the project. Main responsibilities were conducting one-on-one semi-structured user interviews, illustrating design ideas and prototyping.


Duration: 3 weeks


Methods: User Interview, Persona Creation, Wireframes, Prototyping


Tools: Adobe XD, Miro, Zoom

RESEARCH

User Research Outcomes


We conducted 5 one-on-one semi-structured user interviews over Zoom and surveys on Google Forms and Facebook to acquire a broader range of responses.


The key findings derived from the interviews and surveys:

Sharing commonalities such as common interests or hobbies play an important role in forming a new social connection with people both online and offline.
People are concerned about online security and privacy issues.
People feel exhausted to spend a lot of time and effort in finding the right person to talk to online.
Comfortable chatting and not being awkward is important for people when they first start to chat with fresh faces.

Competitor Analysis

Based on the user interview and survey insights, our team had some initial ideas for the eBuddy app. Our next step was to turn to the competition and see how we perform compared to other social networking apps.

From our analysis, it was evident how our competitors allow their users to create user profiles and login/sign upwithin the app. These and other features allow us to take into consideration for our design concepts.

DEFINITION

Problem Statement


ebuddy is designed to help mentally exhausted active social media users effectively and efficiently connect to people who share commonalities such as similar interests or passions with them since we have observed that people have spent a lot of time and effort struggling to search for specific interest matches online.


How might we shorten the process in connecting people who share similar interests, passions or goals so that they can save time and mental effort finding compatible connections online?

TESTING

Usability Testing


We created a test plan for users to complete consecutive tasks on the low-fidelity prototype.


  • - 5 users, 4 tasks.
  • - 100% success rate.

We gained the following insights:

  • - Two participants struggled a bit to find the match button.
  • - One participant commented the heart icon did not signify if they are likes options for a specific feature or to match with someone.
  • - Two participants clicked the setting icon instead of editing profile to verify the account.
